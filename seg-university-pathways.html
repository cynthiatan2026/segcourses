How the University Pathway Programme (UPP) works

Through partnerships with local universities, eligible NYP students can secure early conditional offers and/or module exemptions. In your final semester (Year 3, Semester 2), you get one of two options:

Option 1 — University modules. Take modules offered by NUS, NTU, SMU or SIT in place of NYP learning units.
Option 2 — Immersion project. Complete a 12-week final-year project co-mentored by SUTD professors (SUTD track only).

Related pathways worth knowing

UPP isn't the only route into university-level work while still at NYP.

University Foundation Programme (UFP) NEW

Offered only in Year 3, Semester 2. Take up to three learning units under UFP for 12 credit units, in place of your final-year project — builds critical thinking, professional writing, and pre-university subject knowledge to prepare for matriculation.

Diploma-Plus Programmes

Start as early as Year 1, Semester 2. Complete specific degree-level modules at NYP to sharpen your competitiveness for university admission — you graduate with a Dip-Plus Transcript that may also exempt you from selected modules at NUS or NTU. Applicable to selected diplomas only.

University Preparatory Mentorship Programme

From Year 2 through to one year after graduation, get dedicated mentorship for university interviews, portfolio development, scholarship applications, and the transition into university life.

Source: NYP's University Pathways page. Eligibility and module counts are set by NYP and its university partners and may change — always confirm current details with NYP School of Engineering before making decisions.
